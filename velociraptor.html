Origami Velociraptor

This origami is my first proper origami design. I was inspired by the origami designs of Shuki Kato's origami giganotosaurus, Junha Park's giganotosaurus, and Tran Trung Hieu's velociraptor. I wanted to capture the dynapic aspect of Hieu's design while implementing teeth.

I started by prototyping individual parts of the body. Using box pleating and referencing the crease patterns of Kato and Park's giganotosauruses, I was able to create a rough concept of the head and arms.

After, I drew a crease pattern in Oriedita.

Using the crease pattern, I folded a draft with tracing paper before using nicer, handmade paper.

Final fold using Origamido paper and shaped with methyl cellulose.

The process of designing origami was very challenging, especially connecting the pieces and getting them to work together in a single square. I debated using a smaller grid and rearranging the arms so that they extending from the opposite corner of the paper and not adjacent to the jaw, but that ended up making the paper too short proportionally with the tail and legs. I hope to update the design so that its more efficient and lies fully flat without internal adjustments, alongside more proportionally accurate as the head in the final design turned out much larger than I had expected in proportion to the rest of the body. However, as my first attempt designing an origami, I'm especially pleased with how it came out in relation to how I had envisioned.

Owlbear


This design was inspired by a sugar glider I saw at a zoo. I had been thinking about making a creature design already and I thought the fuzziness and big bulgy eyes of the sugar gliders I had seen were super cute and I wanted to make a design that would capture that feeling.

With my dream game being a Pokemon inspired creature capturing game, I wanted to make a design that had a bit of lore behind it. Given my gripe with how games never explain how saving and checkpoints somehow reset the world, I settled on making it a creature that could control time. The idea of controlling time ended up driving a lot of the design elements.

I first sketched my ideas for a design, trying to get a general shape and gesture down for the design. To fit the theme of time, I used elements from generally prehistoric feeling animals such as birds, in this case owls (because of their large eyes) and raptors such as the velociraptor. Since I still wanted to pay homage to my initial inspiration in sugar gliders, I tried to fit a design of "wing" feathers found in raptors as a sort of wing flap and also made the hands in the shape of sugar a sugar gliders.


Final PureRef board I used for reference.


Blockout of the model based on sketches and reference. I struggled at first with getting the facial form to look as I envisioned but I eventually got it close after gathering more references.


I sculpted together the parts and added details to the face to wrap around the eyes. Sculpting eye sockets made me recognize some problems with my initial sketches and blockout, namely the face not being flat enough. I had to make more changes sculpting than I had anticipated, and as a result, the result at the end of sculpting had a lot of the cute feeling I was trying to capture.


I retopologized with RetopoFlow, adjusting proportions as I went. I was much more satisfied with how the proportions looked after adjusting.


After modeling, I added fur with the Blender hair curves system and textured with a combination of procedural textures and texture painting.
